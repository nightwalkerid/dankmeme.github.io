Pratik Pawar

Science journalist based in India

Pratik Pawar is a freelance science reporter who covers stories about global health, medicine, ecology, and science policy. He is particularly interested in understanding and covering the role inequality and socio-political realities play in furthering health crises. His stories appear in Undark magazine, Discover, Science, and The Atlantic, among other outlets. He is currently based in Bangalore, India.

Pratik was an early-career science writing fellow at The Open Notebook in 2022. Before that, in 2020, he won the EurekAlert! Fellowship for International Science Reporters awarded by the American Association for the Advancement of Science. He was also a recipient of the S. Ramaseshan Fellowship awarded by the Indian Academy of Sciences in 2019.

Prior to writing, Pratik earned a Master’s degree in biotechnology in 2018. If you’d like to send a tip, chat, or just say hi, drop him an email.

It took 35 years to get a malaria vaccine. Why?
At a time when Covid-19 vaccines were developed and authorized in less than one year, the delay for malaria raises an obvious question: Why did a vaccine for a leading global killer take so long to arrive? Undark Magazine, May 2022. Republished by: The Atlantic, Smithsonian Magazine, Popular Science, Mother Jones
